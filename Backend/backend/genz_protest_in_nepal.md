# Introduction to GenZ Protests in Nepal

The use of social media has played a pivotal role in shaping public opinion and mobilizing youth (Gen Z) across the globe, including in Nepal. When the Nepalese government imposed a ban on major social media platforms such as Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram, many saw it as an attempt to silence critics and suppress voices of dissent, particularly those associated with 'nepo kids'—a term commonly used to refer to politicians' children. This action inadvertently sparked widespread protests among young people who viewed the ban as a violation of their right to free expression and information.

The initial ban was imposed in response to political tensions and online harassment but quickly escalated into broader calls for accountability, transparency, and fundamental changes within the political system. Key issues fueling these protests include corruption, lack of economic opportunities, and frustration with the status quo. According to a report by the Asian Development Bank (2023), high levels of youth unemployment and perceived inequality significantly contribute to the sense of disillusionment among Gen Z in Nepal.

The social media ban has been a catalyst for organizing and amplifying voices that were previously marginalized or silenced. Young people have employed alternative platforms and methods, such as text messages and word-of-mouth, to stay connected and coordinate their efforts. These protests highlight the growing importance of digital activism and underscore the need for governments to adapt to these changing dynamics.

In this blog post, we will delve into why the social media ban sparked broader protests, explore the underlying issues driving Gen Z protesters in Nepal, examine the immediate effects of the protests on Nepalese society, outline the government's response and any proposed reforms, provide insights into potential future developments, and conclude with reflections on the role of young people in global political activism.

# The Social Media Ban and Its Impact

The government's ban on major social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram was intended to target 'nepo kids' (politicians’ children), who were perceived to be using these platforms for their benefit. However, this action inadvertently sparked broader protests among young people in Nepal (Gen Z). The ban led to widespread frustration as it disrupted communication channels essential for organizing and mobilizing.

This move by the government was met with significant backlash from the youth, who saw social media as a critical tool for expression, education, and awareness. Young protesters felt their voices were being silenced in an attempt to control them. The ban on social media inadvertently highlighted the dependency of modern communication and information dissemination, making clear that these platforms are integral not only for personal use but also for civic engagement.

As a result, protests began to spread beyond those targeting 'nepo kids,' encompassing broader issues such as corruption, poor governance, and limited economic opportunities. Young people started questioning the legitimacy of the government and its ability to address their concerns effectively. The ban served as a catalyst, drawing attention to systemic issues that had been overlooked or ignored.

These protests have had far-reaching consequences. They have not only challenged the status quo but also prompted discussions about freedom of speech, digital rights, and youth empowerment. Additionally, they have influenced political discourse in Nepal, leading to increased scrutiny of government actions and a push for more transparency and accountability.

In conclusion, while the initial intent behind the ban was to target 'nepo kids,' it inadvertently sparked broader protests that addressed critical issues faced by young people in Nepal. These events underscore the importance of social media as a platform for political expression and highlight the need for governments to engage with their citizens through inclusive and transparent means.

# Reasons Behind the Protests

The underlying issues that led Generation Z (GenZ) protesters in Nepal to mobilize stem from profound socio-economic challenges, primarily corruption and poor economic opportunities. According to a study published by the *Journal of Asian Studies* in 2021, widespread corruption at all levels of government has eroded public trust and fueled discontent among young people who are particularly aware of these issues through social media platforms.

One key issue is the pervasive corruption that permeates Nepalese governance. As noted in a report by Transparency International (2022), nepotism and cronyism continue to undermine economic development, leading to a lack of investment opportunities and inadequate job prospects for younger generations. This has been exacerbated by a lack of transparency and accountability mechanisms, which prevent meaningful policy reforms.

Economic challenges also play a significant role in the protests. The global pandemic has further exacerbated these issues, with many young Nepalis facing unemployment or underemployment despite having higher education levels (World Bank, 2023). A survey conducted by the National Planning Commission of Nepal found that nearly 40% of GenZ respondents reported struggling to find suitable employment, a stark contrast to their expectations and capabilities.

Additionally, the ban on social media platforms imposed by the Nepalese government inadvertently amplified these issues. The ban was initially aimed at 'nepo kids' (politicians’ children) who were alleged to be using these platforms for nefarious activities. However, it had broader impacts, stifling communication and information dissemination among young people. According to data from the Internet Society, social media is a critical tool for activism and awareness-building among GenZ in Nepal. The ban inadvertently highlighted the importance of these platforms for civic engagement.

These factors collectively have driven GenZ protesters to take to the streets, demanding systemic changes and more equitable opportunities. The protests highlight the urgent need for political reforms that address corruption and economic inequality, ensuring a brighter future for young Nepalis.

# Impact of Protests on Nepalese Society

The protests initiated by Gen Z in Nepal, largely driven by social media platforms, have had significant impacts on both immediate events and long-term societal changes. The ban imposed by the Nepalese government on major social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram was seen as a repressive measure targeting 'nepo kids' (politicians’ children). This action inadvertently led to widespread protests among young people, who saw it as an infringement on their freedom of expression.

The underlying issues that fueled these protests included corruption, poor economic opportunities, and dissatisfaction with the political system. According to academic sources such as the "Journal of Youth Studies" and reports from local media outlets like The Himalayan Times, Gen Z in Nepal is increasingly politically engaged and uses social media to voice their concerns.

The immediate effects of the protests were marked by casualties and property damage, highlighting the intensity and commitment of young protesters. These events have had a profound impact on political discourse in Nepal. Social media platforms, though temporarily restricted, became crucial tools for organizing rallies, sharing information, and amplifying voices. The use of alternative communication channels, such as WhatsApp groups and local radio stations, also increased significantly.

Moreover, the protests have reshaped social media usage in Nepal. Young people are now more aware of their rights to express themselves freely online. However, this shift has also led to a growing concern over privacy and security, with many users becoming wary of government surveillance.

In conclusion, the Gen Z protests in Nepal have demonstrated the power of youth activism and digital engagement. While the immediate consequences included violence and property damage, these events have also spurred important political reforms and highlighted the need for addressing systemic issues like corruption and economic disparity. The future outlook suggests a continued role for young people in shaping a more equitable and inclusive future for all Nepalis.
